1. About the project

Domain

Payment application

UxHack

A platform called UXHack enables you to enhance the user experience of your website, mobile apps, and other digital assets with the assistance of experts in product management and user experience.

I took part in this national level design hackathon organised by UXHack with my teammate Sreenivasan. Were the BHIM Mobile Payment application will be redesigned.

The National Payments Corporation of India created the Unified Payments Interface-based mobile payment app called BHIM. It was introduced on December 30th, 2016 with the goal of facilitating bank-to-bank electronic payments and promoting cashless transactions. Bharat Interface for Money is the official name of BHIM. The National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) created the app specifically for mobile payments.

Problem Statement

UPI based payments have literally exploded over the past couple of years. 3rd Party apps like PhonePe, GPay have pretty much captured the market for UPI based payments, with significant gains in volumes and transactions.

However, one player that has seen a decrease in market share is BHIM. While some of this is attributable to lack of discounts and merchant push, visually and UX wise, BHIM does feel like a step back when compared to the likes of PhonePe, GPay, Paytm and several other neobank apps. This is despite the app having no "functional issues" and being rather easy to use.

So, in this challenge, you need to enhance the overall look and feel of the BHIM app: the focus should be on the screens that form the payment flows, as this is the use case that matters the most to users.

User Persona

Personas are made-up users whose objectives and traits stand in for the requirements of a wider user base. To reflect the main user groups, personas must be created by UX designers through evaluation, research, and creation. These user groups are developed using responses, routines, or characteristics that numerous interviewees have in common.

User Story

A made-up one-sentence story given from the viewpoint of the persona to serve as inspiration for and guidance throughout design processes. With a clear action and benefit, users are succinct and powerful. When you begin creating your product, the user stories you develop will be quite helpful.

In order to make sure you're addressing and resolving the major issues your users can encounter while interacting with your product, user stories can also act as a "list of requirements."

User Journey Map

The simplest kind of trip mapping starts by constructing a timeline from a list of user actions. Then, in order to create a tale, user emotions and feelings are added to the timeline. This narrative has been condensed and streamlined, and it concludes with a visualisation.

Therefore, you might be unsure of how to use a customer journey map once you've built one. A customer journey map's primary goal is to keep the customer and their experience at the forefront of everyone's thoughts. Customers trip maps are frequently displayed in the workplace or made readily available to all workers. This manner, the demands of the user may be consistently taken into account throughout the design process by all staff members.

User Flow

A user flow is a visual representation of the various paths that can be taken when using an app or website, which can be written out or created digitally. The flowchart starts with the consumer's first interaction with the product, and finishes with the final action or result.

User flows also make it simple to describe the product's flow to your stakeholders and provide them a basic idea of how the interface you've designed is supposed to behave in its most efficient form.

By assisting your design team in visualizing how users will interact with the product, you can ensure that everyone is on the same page, resulting in a more productive and enjoyable work environment. User flows depict these potential patterns in a way that allows designers to evaluate the efficiency of the interface they're working on.

About Competitors

PhonePe

One of India's leading e-commerce payment platforms is PhonePe Private Limited. The firm that makes digital wallets was established in December 2015. This website provides services in more than 11 regional Indian languages. As a user, you may use the app to book taxis, reserve hotel rooms, place online food orders, pay for Redbus tickets, and purchase aeroplane tickets.

Currently, Yes Bank is working with the service to power it. A user must provide their registered mobile number to register when downloading PhonePe. A user will then have a UPI ID, which they may use to conduct any transactions, and all the information required for a transaction will be live at that point.

All we have to do is download the PhonePe software to our smartphones, link our bank accounts and phone numbers, and after these have been validated, we can begin using the app to conduct transactions. The associated bank accounts are directly connected to the PhonePe transactions. Additionally, since the PhonePe wallet is connected to a bank account, it does not need to be refilled once again.

Gpay

The Google Pay mobile payment service enables customers to use their Android phones, tablets, or watches to make payments for goods and services in apps, online, and in-person.

PINs, passcodes, and biometrics like 3D face scanning and fingerprint recognition are all options for users to authenticate.

Numerous banks and other payment processors partner with the Google Pay service. Support is specifically requested for Visa, MasterCard, Discover, and American Express credit and debit cards. If users are unclear about their bank's compatibility with Google Pay, they should contact that institution directly. A list of supported banks by nation is also available on the Google Pay user website.

The Noida-based startup Paytm(Pat through mobile) provides financial services and digital payments.

The paytm offers mobile payment services to consumers and enables merchants to receive payments through its QR code, point of sale and online payment gateway offerings. In partnership with financial institutions, Paytm offers financial services such as microloans and buy now, pay later to its consumers and merchants. Apart from bill payments and money transfer, the company also provides ticketing services, retail brokerage products and online games.

Paytm's parent company, One97 Communications Limited, got listed on Indian stock exchanges on November 18, 2021 after an initial public offering, which was the largest in India at the time. For the fiscal year 2021-22, Paytm's gross merchandise value was reported to be ₹8,500 billion .

4. Starting the Design

Paper Wireframes

A wireframe is a low-fidelity outline of a digital experience, like an app or website.

Purposes of wireframes:

• Establish the basic structure of a page

• Highlight the intended function of the product

• Save time and resources

The wireframes will be made up of elements. Elements are building blocks used for creating a design. Most of the time, you'll use lines, shapes, and text to draw wireframes.

• Text is represented by horizontal lines.

• Images, photos, illustrations, and icons are represented by a square or rectangle and an X overlapping the square or rectangle.

• Calls-to-action are often represented by rectangles or circles. A common example of a call-to-action is a “submit” button on a web form.

Wireframes benefit you as the designer; they enable you to:

• Identify the content to include in your product

• Catch problems early

• Get stakeholders to focus on structure

• Save time and effort

• Iterate quickly

Low Fidelity Wireframes

A quick and simple method for turning high-level design concepts into observable and testable prototypes is low-fidelity (lo-fi) prototyping. Lo-fi prototypes' primary and most crucial job is to test and verify functioning, not the product's aesthetic appeal.

Here are the basic characteristics of low-fidelity prototyping:

Visual design: Only a few of the final product's visual characteristics are displayed (such as shapes of elements, basic visual hierarchy, etc.).

Content: Only the most important parts of the content are presented.

Interactivity: A real person can imitate the prototype. During a testing session, a specific person who is knowledgeable about design assumes the role of a computer and manually modifies the design's state in real-time. Wireframes, commonly referred to as "connected wireframes" can be used to generate interactivity.

5.Refining the Design

High Fidelity Wireframes

The degree of features and functionality incorporated into a prototype is referred to as the fidelity of the prototype. In this sense, a high-fidelity prototype is an interactive computer-based depiction of the product that comes the closest to the final design in terms of features and functionality. It is sometimes referred to as high-fidelity or hi-fidelity. High-fidelity refers to a level of comprehensiveness that enables in-depth analysis of usability issues and the drawing of inferences on user behaviour.

The high-fidelity prototypes encompass both the user experience (UX) and user interface (UI) components of the product, including interactions, user flow, and user behaviour.

Transactions

Based on the user's prior transactions, the data is presented in a way that makes it simple for the user to comprehend their transactions.

Based on the user's decision, they are divided into many categories. Because they could readily understand by skimming, many people cherished this feature. Many users sought information on the topic of expenditures, which led to the payments being categorised.

   

Wallet

Since the current version of BHIM doesn't have a wallet function, it has been freshly included into the application based on user preference and competitive analysis.

Many people find the usage of wallet helpful in difficult circumstances. It holds funds that may be transferred from and to your bank account. The wallet on BHIM may be transferred to multiple bank accounts in addition to moving money from one user's wallet to another.

The BHIM wallet also includes a passbook where users may track their spending and, like with transactions, establish spending limits. This passbook is shown in a bar chart style so users can have a more detailed view of their daily transactions.
